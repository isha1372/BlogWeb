The Early Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

IS Ust. Ibraheem Saleem · Seerah · 09 June 2026

Long before revelation began, the story of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was already one of loss, resilience, and quiet preparation. Born in Makkah in the Year of the Elephant, he lost his father, Abdullah, before he was even born, and his mother, Aminah, passed away when he was only six years old. By the time he was eight, his grandfather Abdul Muttalib — who had taken on his care — had also passed.

Sent to the desert

Following a custom among the noble families of Makkah, the infant Muhammad ﷺ was sent to live with a Bedouin foster family, the Banu Saad, under the care of Halimah al-Sa'diyyah. This was common practice — children were sent to the desert to grow up away from the crowded city, learning pure Arabic and developing strength and self-reliance in a harsher environment.

Halimah later recalled that from the moment the infant Muhammad ﷺ arrived in her household, their flocks gave more milk and their land yielded more than it had before — a sign, even then, of the blessing that accompanied him.

A childhood shaped by loss and care

After his mother's passing, his uncle Abu Talib took him in and raised him as his own, despite Abu Talib's own modest means. It was in this household — surrounded by people who, despite hardship, treated him with warmth — that the young Muhammad ﷺ grew into a young man known throughout Makkah for his honesty, even before any of the Quraysh knew what was to come.

Even in these early years, before prophethood, we can already see patterns that would define his later life: a tendency toward reflection, a reputation for trustworthiness, and a life marked again and again by loss met with patience.
