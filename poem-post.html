An Ode to the Morning Adhan

SN Sahla Nazrin · Poem · 11 June 2026

There is a particular kind of quiet that settles over a street just before the Fajr adhan — streetlights still on, the sky not yet decided between night and day. This poem was written during one of those mornings, in that pause before the call begins.

Before the light has chosen its colour,
before the street remembers its noise,
a voice climbs over rooftops —
not loud, only certain —
and the city, for a moment,
holds its breath with the believer.

It does not ask if you are ready.
It does not wait for the kettle to boil.
It simply arrives, the way mercy does,
early, and again,
and again.

On writing this poem

I wanted to capture the adhan not as an event, but as a presence — something that arrives whether or not we feel ready for it, much like guidance itself. The repetition in the final lines is deliberate: the adhan comes again and again, every day, regardless of how we responded to it yesterday. There is comfort in that consistency.
