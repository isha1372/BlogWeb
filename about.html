About Risala

Faith, knowledge, and reflection — written for everyday readers.

Risala began as a simple idea: that reminders of faith shouldn't feel distant or difficult to reach. Whether it's a short reflection on a verse from the Quran, a story passed down through generations, a question of everyday Fiqh, or a few lines of poetry written from the heart — we wanted a single, calm place to gather it all.

The name "Risala" means a message or letter — a small communication carrying something meaningful from one person to another. That's exactly how we think of every article published here: a short letter, written with care, meant to be read slowly and reflected upon.

What you'll find here

Our content is organised into five gentle categories. Fiqh articles look at the practical side of worship and daily life. Quran reflections explore the meaning and context behind verses and surahs. Seerah pieces walk through the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, chapter by chapter. Poem and Story sections offer a softer, more creative way to connect with the same themes — in both English and Malayalam.

Clarity

We write in plain, accessible language — for readers encountering a topic for the first time as well as those returning to it.

Care

Every article is written and reviewed with attention to accuracy and tone, especially on matters of faith.

Community

Readers are welcome to ask questions, leave comments, and write to us directly — Risala is meant to be a conversation, not a lecture.

A publication of Gazva Media

Risala is published and maintained by Gazva Media, a small media collective focused on thoughtful, faith-centred content. If you'd like to know more about our other projects, or get in touch about a collaboration, you can reach us through the contact page — we read every message.
